Insights & Fixes
Patterns detected from your 2025–2026 data
Key findings
DE is the #1 problem store. 494 issues — 238 are wrong/missing address (48% of DE cases from one fixable checkout problem).
Korea ID is 100% preventable. 112 identical cases. Adding a 12-digit personal ID field at .kr checkout eliminates all of them.
Spain remote area is disproportionate. 49 of 70 global remote cases are .es — a postcode blocklist at checkout stops these before they ship.
DE has 72 recipient name issues. Likely DHL character limits. Name validation at checkout would prevent courier rejections.
FI & SE dominate failed pickups. 223 + 152 cases. Automated pickup reminders reduce "no response" significantly.
Q4 volume triples. Oct (219), Nov (134), Dec (227) vs ~40/month in Q1–Q2. Pre-hire before September.
Customs cases almost always resolve. 210 of 212 delivered (99%). Low risk.
Undelivered resolution is strong. 256 of 278 resolved (92%). Your team handles these well.
Recommended actions
1. Address autocomplete for DE, NL, PL. 324 combined address cases. Google Places autocomplete at checkout solves most instantly.
2. Korea 12-digit ID field at .kr checkout. Collect before order placement. Single easiest win in the dataset.
3. Block remote postcodes at .es checkout. DHL ES or Correos can provide the unserviceable postcode list.
4. Scale CS team in September. Hiring reactively in November means you're already behind.
5. Automated pickup reminders for FI & SE. Email or SMS after 2 days at parcel locker.
6. Separate OOS into its own tracker. 150 OOS cases skew your shipping metrics.
7. DHL DE name character limit. Set 35-char max with format guidance at the name field.
